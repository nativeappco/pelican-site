Shoppers are increasingly starting their journey with an AI assistant instead of
a search bar. When an agent decides which products to surface, it doesn't look at
your theme, your hero image, or your carefully tuned PDP layout. It reads your
**structured product data** — titles, descriptions, attributes, taxonomy, and
schema. If that data is thin, inconsistent, or missing, your products are
invisible to the buyer's agent.

## What "agent-readable" actually means

Three things move the needle for Generative Engine Optimization (GEO):

1. **Complete attributes.** SKUs, barcodes, materials, dimensions, and images on
   every variant — the fields agents use to match intent to product.
2. **Native taxonomy.** Products mapped to Shopify's standard product taxonomy so
   agents can reason about categories, not guess from free text.
3. **Structured markup.** Valid `Product` and `FAQ` schema so machines can parse
   price, availability, and answers without scraping prose.

## Why do it inside Shopify

Bolt-on PIMs pull your catalog into a separate system, add a sync layer, and give
you one more place for data to drift. Product Pelican runs **natively in the
Shopify admin** — it audits your catalog where it lives, enriches gaps in place,
and keeps taxonomy and schema aligned with the source of truth. No export, no
round-trip, no reconciliation.
