AI · Vision

Image alt text, written properly.

Pelican looks at every product image and writes alt text that actually describes what's there — colour, material, context. Generate one at a time, generate all in bulk, edit the suggestion before saving. Better for screen readers, better for Google Images, better for the AI agents now crawling your storefront.

Generate. Apply. Or save all. Your call, every time.

Native · Taxonomy

Shopify taxonomy, finally done.

Suggest categories from Shopify's official taxonomy for every product missing one. Confidence scores tell you which ones are bulletproof and which need a glance. Powers better search, filters, Shop and Google integration. Done manually, this is a multi-week project. Done in Pelican, it's an afternoon.

The native unlock. Done right, this powers half the platform.

AI · Content

Titles & descriptions, on brand.

Spot vague titles, short titles, missing descriptions, weak descriptions, ambiguous language, unclear options — then rewrite them in your brand voice. Grounded in your existing product data, images and taxonomy. Not hallucinated.

Guard rails on. Preview & approve before anything ships.

Audit · Health

Product Health, at a glance.

One dashboard. Active products, total variants, products with issues, clean products. Twelve audit checks underneath: no images, only one image, missing alt text, no category, vague titles, short titles, no description, short description, ambiguous language, unclear options, missing SKUs, zero-price variants. Switch to GEO Score for the agent-readiness view.

Diagnose & fix. Free scanners stop at the report. We don't.

Workflow · Alt text

Generate, review, apply — or save all.

Each image card shows the product, the filename, the current issue, the AI suggestion, and a character count. Edit the suggestion in place, accept it, regenerate it, or copy it.

Hit Generate All for bulk suggestions. Hit Save All when you're confident. The whole thing is built around merchant control — Pelican never changes anything you haven't approved.

Workflow · Categories

Confidence, not guesswork.

Every category suggestion comes with a confidence rating — High, Medium, or worth-a-second-look. Run Suggest All across thousands of products in one go.

The high-confidence ones can be applied in bulk without a glance. The medium ones get a quick review. The job that used to take a week takes an afternoon.

Colour options grouped. "Pair it with" pairs surfaced. "Complete the set" sets stitched. Pelican reads your catalogue's signals — names, SKUs, attributes, taxonomy — and builds the relationships natively in Shopify.

Better cross-sell, better upsell, better discoverability. Native to the platform, not bolted on through a recommendation widget that breaks the moment your theme changes.

Missing SKUs. Missing barcodes. Missing images. The kind of stuff that sits quietly broken in every catalogue until ChatGPT recommends a competitor's product instead of yours.

/ 02 AI Prompts

You write the rules. Pelican follows them.

Pelican's AI isn't a black box. Every prompt — for titles, descriptions, alt text, categories — is yours to edit. Add brand voice. Add shipping messaging. Add merchandising rules. Add what to never say.

  • Title promptTone, structure, vendor handling.
  • Description promptVoice, shipping rules, USPs.
  • Alt text promptDetail level, accessibility tone.
  • Category promptRestrictions, preferences, exceptions.

Update the prompt, regenerate the suggestions, ship. Reset to defaults whenever you want.

/ 05 The story

How this got built.

"

I didn't set out to build a PIM. I was vibe-coding a few product audits for Shopify merchants — completeness checks, taxonomy gaps, SEO holes. Then I added AI to fix what the audits found. Then bulk actions. Then stitching. Then governance.

One day I looked at it and went: oh shit, I've accidentally built a PIM — and it lives 100% inside Shopify.

Turns out the right way to build one was never to build a separate system. It was to build it where the data already lives — with proper AI guard rails, and ultimate control left in the merchant's hands.
