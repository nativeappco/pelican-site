New · Gap fixes

The data debt, finally paid off.

Missing SKUs. Missing barcodes. Missing images. The kind of stuff that sits quietly broken in every catalogue until ChatGPT recommends a competitor's product instead of yours.

Pelican spots the gaps, suggests sensible fixes — pulled from product data, vendor data, variant data — and applies them in bulk. The boring work, automated.

New features shipping every week.

This is the bit that gets us excited. Native Shopify automation that nobody else can ship — because nobody else lives where the data lives.

New

Merchandising rules.

Merchandise Rulesets sort your products by what matters, promote your featured tags, push out-of-stock and image-less products to the bottom, then re-sort automatically every few hours. Every ruleset is stored as a native Shopify metaobject, so the rules live in your store, not ours.

Native unlock. A merchandiser that never sleeps.

New

Collection audits.

You've been auditing products. Now Pelican audits the collections too. Empty collections. Thin collections. Missing descriptions. Missing images. The stuff that quietly tanks a campaign and that nobody notices until a customer does. Every problem, one screen, fix it before it goes live.

Same workflow. Now one level up.

New

Collection content, populated.

Sixty collections and eight have a description? Pelican writes the other fifty-two in one pass, on brand, with your shipping and USP rules baked in, SEO and metafields included. The collection-level content gaps your audit just found, filled in bulk. No spreadsheet. No copywriter queue.

The boring work. Automated, again.

New

Product stitching.

Automatically link related products — colour options, "pair it with", "complete the set". Pelican reads your catalogue and builds the relationships for you, the way a good merchandiser would. Cross-sells, upsells and discoverability without a single manual link.

Native unlock. Shopify product relationships, automated.

New

Category metafields in bulk.

Shopify's product taxonomy is incredible — but the category metafields underneath it are a minefield. Pelican now populates them automatically in bulk, perfectly aligned with the official taxonomy. Better filters, better search, better Shop, better Google integration. All native.

Headache avoided. Using AI the way it's meant to be used.

New

Bulk gap fixes.

Missing SKUs. Missing barcodes. Missing images. The data debt every catalogue carries quietly until something breaks. Pelican now finds the gaps, suggests the fixes, and applies them at scale. Less spreadsheet archaeology, more shipping.

Three audits. One workflow. Done.

Time back

How this actually saves you time.

Not vibes. Specifics.

Collection descriptions for the whole store.

You've got 60 collections and a handful have copy. Writing the rest by hand is a day you'll never actually schedule, so it never happens. Pelican flags every empty one and writes them all in a single pass, on brand, shipping and USPs included.

A day's work. Done over a coffee.

Re-merchandising that doesn't need re-doing.

Sorting a collection by hand lasts until the next restock, then it's wrong again. Set a Merchandise Ruleset once - in-stock up, sold-out down, featured promoted - and it holds every time inventory moves. You stop babysitting the grid.

Set it once. Stop touching it.

The pre-campaign check, in seconds.

Before a sale you'd click through every collection hunting for broken bits: empty grids, missing images, thin copy. Collection Audits lists all of it on one screen, so you fix the campaign before launch instead of after the first complaint email.

An afternoon of clicking. Now one screen.

The merchandising thesis

Your collections are merchandised by accident.

Default Shopify sorting drops sold-out products, image-less products and yesterday's leftovers right where shoppers land. A merchandiser would fix it every morning. Nobody has time to fix it every morning.

So Merchandise Rulesets do it for you: sort, promote, demote, bury the dead stock, and re-sort on a schedule. Native to Shopify, stored as a metaobject you own. Set the rule once. The grid sorts itself, forever.

"Sort order is a merchandising decision. You're either making it, or leaving it to a date field."

The story

How this got built.

"

I didn't set out to build a PIM. I was vibe-coding a few product audits for Shopify merchants — completeness checks, taxonomy gaps, SEO holes. Then I added AI to fix what the audits found. Then bulk actions. Then stitching. Then governance.

One day I realised: oh shit, I've accidentally built a PIM — and it lives 100% inside Shopify.

Turns out the right way to build one was never to build a separate system. It was to build it where the data already lives — with proper AI guard rails, and ultimate control left in the merchant's hands.

Martin Cox  ·  Co-Founder, Native App Co
