A2AAgent2Agent ProtocolProtocol

How one AI agent talks to another AI agent, like your shopping assistant handing off to a payment assistant to finish the job.

Google's protocol for peer to peer communication and task delegation between autonomous agents, used alongside UCP and MCP in the broader commerce stack. Now governed by the Linux Foundation.

ACPAgentic Commerce ProtocolProtocol

OpenAI and Stripe's version of "let AI agents buy stuff," specifically the one that lets a shopper check out directly inside ChatGPT.

A protocol built by OpenAI and Stripe defining how an agent runs a complete checkout, cart through payment, inside a conversational surface like ChatGPT. Distinct from, but interoperable with, UCP.

MCPModel Context ProtocolProtocol

A way for AI models to safely fetch live info or take actions, like checking stock or building a cart, instead of just guessing from old training data.

An open standard (originally from Anthropic, now under the Linux Foundation) defining how AI agents connect to external tools, data, and APIs. Forms the transport layer underneath most commerce protocols, including UCP.

A file on every website that tells crawlers and bots which pages they're allowed to visit, including whether AI bots can read your site at all.

UCPUniversal Commerce ProtocolProtocol

A shared rulebook, built by Google and Shopify with a lot of the industry behind it, so any AI agent can browse and buy from any store the same way, instead of every AI needing a custom setup per store.

An open standard governing discovery, cart, checkout, and post-purchase interactions between AI agents and merchants. Composable with AP2 for payments and MCP or A2A for transport.
